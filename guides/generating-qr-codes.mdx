QR codes are the primary way users claim tokens in your loyalty campaign. This guide explains how to generate, manage, and distribute QR codes for your Stampchain campaigns.

## Overview

Each QR code is a one-time use token that directs users to a claim page where they can receive loyalty tokens to their wallet. The QR code generation system allows you to create batches of codes for distribution to customers.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access QR Code Generator">
    After creating a campaign, you'll see a "Generate QR Codes" button on the success page. You can also access the QR generator at any time by:
    
    1. Navigating to `/qr-generator` from your admin dashboard
    2. Clicking the QR code icon on your campaign details page
    
    **Location in code:** `src/app/(protected-route)/admin/campaign/success/components/GenerateQR.tsx:35`
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Campaign">
    On the QR generator page, you'll see a configuration panel on the left side.

    ### Choose Your Campaign
    From the dropdown menu, select the campaign you want to generate QR codes for. The dropdown shows:
    - Campaign name
    - Token symbol in parentheses
    
    For example: "Coffee Shop Loyalty Program (COFFEE)"

    **Location in code:** `src/app/(protected-route)/qr-generator/components/configuration-panel-form.tsx:111`

    <Note>
      Screenshot placeholder: Campaign selection dropdown with multiple campaigns listed
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Specify Quantity">
    Choose how many QR codes to generate from the quantity dropdown:
    
    - 5 QR Codes
    - 10 QR Codes
    - 25 QR Codes
    - 50 QR Codes
    - 100 QR Codes

    **Location in code:** `src/app/(protected-route)/qr-generator/components/configuration-panel-form.tsx:129`

    <Info>
      Each QR code can only be used once. Plan your quantity based on the number of customers you expect to reach.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate QR Codes">
    Click the "Generate QR Codes" button to create your batch. The system will:
    
    1. Create unique claim tokens for each QR code
    2. Generate claim URLs that include the token
    3. Render QR codes that link to the claim page
    4. Display all generated codes in a grid layout

    The generation process typically takes a few seconds depending on the quantity requested.

    **Location in code:** `src/app/(protected-route)/qr-generator/components/configuration-panel-form.tsx:39`

    <Note>
      Screenshot placeholder: Generated QR codes displayed in a grid with tokens and action buttons
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="View QR Code Details">
    Each generated QR code is displayed in a card showing:

    ### QR Code Number
    Sequential numbering (e.g., "QR Code #1", "QR Code #2")

    ### One-time Use Badge
    A badge indicating the code can only be claimed once

    ### Visual QR Code
    The scannable QR code image that users will scan with their camera

    ### Claim Token
    A unique alphanumeric token in a read-only input field. Users can manually enter this token on the claim page if they can't scan the QR code.

    **Location in code:** `src/app/(protected-route)/qr-generator/components/qr-code-display.tsx:77`
  </Step>

  <Step title="Copy Claim Tokens">
    Each QR code card includes a copy button next to the claim token. Click this button to:
    
    - Copy the token to your clipboard
    - Share the token via text or email
    - Provide backup access if the QR code is damaged

    The button shows a checkmark icon for 2 seconds after copying to confirm the action.

    **Location in code:** `src/app/(protected-route)/qr-generator/components/qr-code-display.tsx:20`
  </Step>

  <Step title="Download Individual QR Codes">
    Each QR code card has a "Download" button that saves the QR code as an image file to your device. This is useful for:
    
    - Printing individual codes
    - Sharing specific codes digitally
    - Creating custom marketing materials

    **Location in code:** `src/app/(protected-route)/qr-generator/components/qr-code-display.tsx:122`
  </Step>

  <Step title="Download All QR Codes">
    At the top of the configuration panel, you'll see a "Download All" button that appears after generating codes. This button:
    
    - Downloads all QR codes in a single ZIP file
    - Names each file sequentially
    - Provides a convenient way to distribute multiple codes

    The button shows the count of codes being downloaded (e.g., "Download All (25)").

    **Location in code:** `src/app/(protected-route)/qr-generator/components/configuration-panel-form.tsx:162`

    <Note>
      Screenshot placeholder: Download All button at the top showing the count of generated codes
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Test QR Codes">
    Before distributing your QR codes, test them to ensure they work correctly:

    1. Click the "Test Claim" button on any QR code card
    2. You'll be redirected to the claim page with the token pre-filled
    3. Follow the claim process to verify it works

    **Location in code:** `src/app/(protected-route)/qr-generator/components/qr-code-display.tsx:132`

    <Warning>
      Testing a QR code will mark it as used. Generate extra codes for testing purposes, or test with a separate wallet.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Distribution Methods

Once you've generated and downloaded your QR codes, there are several effective ways to distribute them to customers:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Physical Print" icon="print">
    Print QR codes on receipts, business cards, posters, table tents, or stickers. Place them where customers can easily scan them.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Digital Sharing" icon="share-nodes">
    Share QR codes via email, SMS, social media, or messaging apps. Include the visual code and the backup claim token.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Event Distribution" icon="calendar">
    Display QR codes at events, conferences, or pop-up shops. Use large posters or digital displays for easy scanning.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Product Packaging" icon="box">
    Include QR codes on product packaging, labels, or inserts. This rewards customers at the point of purchase.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## QR Code Management

### Viewing Existing QR Codes

The QR generator automatically loads previously generated codes for the selected campaign. When you select a campaign from the dropdown, all existing QR codes are fetched and displayed.

**Location in code:** `src/app/(protected-route)/qr-generator/components/qr-code-display.tsx:30`

### Tracking QR Code Usage

QR codes show their status:
- **Available**: Ready to be claimed (indicated by "One-time use" badge)
- **Used**: Already claimed (tracked in the database)

You can monitor usage statistics in the [Analytics](/guides/analytics) section of your admin dashboard.

### Generating Additional Codes

You can generate more QR codes for a campaign at any time:

1. Return to the QR generator
2. Select the same campaign
3. Choose additional quantity
4. Click "Generate QR Codes"

New codes are added to the existing pool and displayed alongside previous codes.

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Print Quality">
    When printing QR codes:
    - Use high resolution (300 DPI minimum)
    - Ensure sufficient size (at least 2cm × 2cm)
    - Test scanning from various distances
    - Avoid glossy surfaces that create glare
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Backup Access">
    Always provide the claim token as backup:
    - Include it in small print below the QR code
    - Share it alongside digital QR codes
    - Keep a master list of tokens and their distribution locations
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Security Considerations">
    Protect your QR codes from fraud:
    - Don't share QR codes publicly online (one person could claim them all)
    - Limit distribution to verified customers
    - Monitor analytics for unusual claiming patterns
    - Generate codes in batches as needed rather than all at once
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Strategic Placement">
    Place QR codes where they provide value:
    - High-traffic areas with good lighting
    - Near checkout counters or point of sale
    - On receipts after purchase
    - At the completion of a service
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

### QR Codes Not Loading

If previously generated QR codes don't appear:
1. Verify you've selected the correct campaign
2. Check your internet connection
3. Refresh the page
4. Ensure the campaign exists and is active

### Download Failures

If downloads fail:
1. Check browser permissions for file downloads
2. Ensure sufficient disk space
3. Try downloading individual codes instead of batch
4. Clear browser cache and try again

### Scanning Issues

If customers report scanning problems:
1. Test the QR code with multiple devices
2. Verify the QR code isn't damaged or distorted
3. Ensure adequate lighting when scanning
4. Provide the backup claim token

## Technical Details

### QR Code Format

Each QR code encodes a URL in the format:
```
https://your-domain.com/claim?token=UNIQUE_TOKEN_ID
```

The token is a unique identifier that links to:
- The campaign ID
- Token allocation details
- Usage status

### Claim Process

When a user scans a QR code:
1. Their camera app opens the claim URL
2. The claim page validates the token
3. User connects their wallet
4. Tokens are transferred to their wallet
5. QR code is marked as used

See [Claiming Rewards](/guides/claiming-rewards) for the complete user flow.
